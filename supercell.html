⭐ What is a Supercell? A cell is a supercell if its number is greater than ALL its adjacent (neighbouring) cells. Adjacent means sharing a side — left, right (and in a grid: top, bottom too). End cells have only 1 neighbour.
🏆Largest number is ALWAYS a supercell — it beats every neighbour!
🚫Smallest number can NEVER be a supercell — neighbours always beat it!
🔗Two adjacent cells can NEVER both be supercells at the same time!
📊Max supercells in n cells = ⌈n/2⌉ (every alternate cell).
