Superimposition & Circular Paper Method
📖 From Ganit Prakash Chapter 2: We can compare two angles WITHOUT measuring their degrees. There are two main methods — Superimposition and the Circular Paper Method.

🔁 Method 1 — Superimposition (Placing One on Top of Another)

Draw two angles on paper (or tracing paper).
Match the vertices — place both angles so their corners (vertices) are exactly on top of each other.
Match one arm — align one ray of each angle together.
Look at the other arm — the angle whose second arm is outside/further is the bigger angle!
If both second arms overlap perfectly → the angles are equal.

💡 Key idea: The angle that "sticks out more" after superimposing is the greater one.

⭕ Method 2 — Circular Paper (Without Superimposition)

Take a circular piece of transparent paper (or trace a circle).
Place its centre on the vertex of the first angle.
Mark the two points where the arms of the angle cross the circle.
Now place the same circle on the second angle (centre on vertex).
Compare the marked arc — the angle whose arc is longer is bigger!

💡 Key idea: A bigger angle "uses up" more of the circle. The arc it cuts is longer.

📏 Important Rules to Remember

✅ Size of arms doesn't matter! An angle with short arms can still be bigger than one with long arms. Only the opening/spread between the arms counts.

✅ Position doesn't matter! An angle tilted sideways is the same size as the same angle standing upright.

✅ Both methods give the same answer — one just needs both angles together (superimposition), the other works separately (circular method).
